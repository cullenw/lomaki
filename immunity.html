Why This Matters

  • More than 35 million people now live with HIV/AIDS. 3.3 million of them are under the age of 15.
  • Every day nearly 6,300 people contract HIV—nearly 262 every hour.
  • Over 4,000 people die a day from AIDS
  • More than 75 million people have contracted HIV and nearly 36 million have died of HIV-related causes.

Why Lomaki Picked Immunity

Developing an HIV/AIDS vaccine has been the holy grail of the medical community for decades, and yet little progress has been made. Immunity's informatics-based approach represents a paradigm shift.

If successful, the vaccine wouldn't just manage the virus -- it would actually stop it. Lives would be saved on a scale never seen before with HIV/AIDS, regardless of who they are and where they live.

Let's cure HIV/AIDS together.

Overview

The Immunity Project is trying to develop a free nasal HIV/AIDS vaccine with a radical new approach that involves "hacking" the lifecycle of the virus.

The Work

One out of every three hundred people with HIV/AIDS has a natural immunity to the virus -- known as "controllers." Immunity uses advanced data analytics and artificial intelligence to identify how controllers keep the virus dormant. It is designing a vaccine that could give anyone the immunity power of a controller.

The vaccine will not require refrigeration, only requires one dosage, and can be delivered with a nasal inhaler -- all characteristics that address critical distribution challenges in countries with high HIV infection rates. It also has the potential to act preventatively.

“This is the ultimate application of informatics to medicine. So much vaccine design since 1953 has been based on neutralizing antibodies, but that legacy approach doesn’t work for HIV. The virus is too smart and can mutate so quickly. We are doing something very different.”

Your Impact

The Immunity Project still has years of work, experimentation, clinical trials, and federal approvals before the vaccine can get to market. It is raising $25 million right now to begin Phase 1 FDA human trials, which it hopes to begin in December 2014. The third and final phase of clinical trials and widespread vaccination efforts won’t happen before 2016. Every dollar donated goes directly towards getting this vaccine to the people who need it.

The Story

The Immunity Project is a partnership between medical device company Flow Pharma and digital agency Sparkart. CEO Dr. Reid Rubsamen previously founded Aradigm Corporation and has 65 patents for drug delivery technologies.

Dr. David Heckermann initially developed Immunity’s algorithm at Microsoft e-Science Research. It is based on similar principles to spam filtering software, because both spam and HIV spread rapidly, mutate relentlessly, and have a multitude of variations.

Immunity is one of the few nonprofits to be accepted into yCombinator’s elite startup accelerator program.
